# Ethiopian Compliance Review Readiness

This document is a product-readiness checklist for Hisab ERP. It is not legal, tax, accounting or regulatory advice and does not certify compliance. Requirements vary by entity type, tax status, reporting tier, workforce and regulated sector.

## Suggested review team

Obtain written review from the professionals relevant to the customer and deployment:

- Ethiopian licensed accountant or tax adviser
- External auditor or reporting-framework adviser
- Payroll, PAYE and pension adviser
- Ethiopian privacy and technology counsel
- Electronic invoicing or fiscal-device implementation specialist
- Sector regulator or licensed sector consultant where applicable
- Customer finance owner who accepts the configured business rules

## 1. Entity and tax registration

Confirm the legal name, trade name, TIN, registered address, business licence, VAT or TOT status and every registered branch. Ensure protected legal fields are change-controlled and visible on the required documents.

## 2. Sales invoices and tax documents

Review invoice numbering, required buyer and seller information, issue date, description, tax treatment, totals, currency and correction documents. Confirm whether the business must use an approved e-invoice, electronic fiscal device or approved accounting-software process.

Tax codes should have effective dates, clear input or output treatment and controlled changes. Test invoices, credit notes, returns and receipts before launch.

## 3. Accounting and financial reporting

Confirm the entity's reporting tier and applicable framework with the Accounting and Auditing Board of Ethiopia requirements. Review the chart of accounts, opening balances, fiscal periods, period locks, double-entry validation, financial statements and audit evidence.

## 4. Records, audit and recovery

Confirm the required retention period and acceptable electronic record format. Review immutable posting history, document references, audit exports, user attribution, approval evidence, encrypted backups and periodic isolated restore tests.

## 5. Payroll and employment records

Obtain local review of payroll calculations, PAYE, pension, allowances, deductions, payslips, employee identifiers, attendance, leave and employment-record retention. Restrict payroll and employee data to authorized roles.

## 6. Privacy and personal data

Review lawful processing, notices, purpose limitation, access controls, retention, correction, deletion where applicable, processor terms, cross-border handling, incident response and auditability. Map which ERP fields contain employee, customer, supplier or user personal data.

## 7. Electronic transactions

Review electronic consent, signatures, acknowledgements, record integrity, time evidence and the ability to reproduce electronic records. Confirm that automated communications and digital approvals produce appropriate evidence.

## 8. Role and security controls

Review least-privilege roles, separation of duties, administrator MFA, session controls, sensitive-action confirmation, export permissions and periodic access review. Verify that role-based dashboards do not grant permissions beyond the underlying authorization rules.

## 9. Sector-specific obligations

Banks, insurers, health providers, schools, nonprofits, construction companies, importers, exporters and other regulated businesses may require additional licences, records, reports or regulator approval. Complete sector review before describing a deployment as compliant.

## Evidence to retain

For each customer deployment, retain:

- Reviewer names, qualifications and dates
- Entity and tax-registration evidence
- Accepted invoice and tax configuration
- Reporting framework and chart-of-accounts decision
- Payroll and privacy decisions
- Test results and approved exceptions
- Application version and Git commit SHA reviewed
- Final customer acceptance and re-review date

Re-run the review after material legal changes, new regulated workflows, new countries, major tax changes or significant ERP architecture changes.
